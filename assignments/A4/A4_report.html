Average Poverty Headcount Ratio by Country

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • First, note that this data starts from 2010, which is not much. Also, note that many countries do not have this data, including many from Latin America which is our Proposition.
  • From the little data we have, we can see that the areas with the highest average poverty ratio are mainly Latin America and Africa and the area with the lowest average poverty ratio is Europe

Average Population Living in Slums as a percentage

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • First, note that this data starts from 1990 which is more representative than the last graph. Also, note that some big countries do not have this data, but thankfully those are outside Latin America.
  • We can see that the area with the highest population living in slums is mainly Africa, in this case we see also Asia as a big one and the area with the lowest population living in slums is Europe and Canada having some places with 0.0 and 0.1 percent.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • First, I had to look up what the gini index meant, and making this graph gave a good estimate over the years using the average function. The gini index is a good way to see the inequality in the income of the population of a country
  • High gini index is a big factor that contributes to poverty since an income inequality will cause people with less resources to have to settle for less since they won't have enough money compared to people that have way more money
  • We can see that the area with the highest gini index is Latin America and the area with the lowest gini index is Europe

Deceptive or Earnest (-2 to 2): For this visualization, I would give it a -0.5 since it is hard to compare Latin America to Africa with only using colors which is part of the deception since the reader should trust that what I am saying is right. I thought of not using countries that could affect the Latin America argument, but I thought that doing it is not good practice

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • The mean shortfall for income or consumption is when you make X amount of dollars less than what you need, in this case X being $5.50 a day. This number, to be a daily thing, is huge and is definitely an indicator of poverty rates in a country.
  • Think about it like this, $5.50 a day is a total of $2007.50 a year which is a big amount for poor people. This number, which was calculated as the average of the percentage, indicates the depth of the poverty of the country
  • Using this statistic, we can see that Africa is the poorest region, followed by Asia, and then Latin America.

Deceptive or Earnest (-2 to 2): For this visualization, I would give it a 2 since it is easy to see that Africa and Asia has a higher percentage in average than Latin America. I believe this option was a pretty strong one since the beginning

Final Reflection

This assignment has been an eye-opener project. It first showed me how easy it is to take data and "push it your way" by using the data in the specific way you want and without showing the flaws. I imagine many non-serious news outlets use this type of practice to spread data that is not real. Knowing how to bend words and data can make anyone believable and a "weapon" of misinformation. This assignment was difficult to complete since poverty is definitely not only one of the categories from the dataset, it is a mix of all of them. As a matter of fact, I do not believe poverty has a specific definition data-wise. Poverty is a mix of different statistics, and it is hard to say that one region is the poorest of them all.

I choose poverty for this project because poverty shouldn't be a term in the sense that everyone should be able to live without the need to be in a poverty standard. I believe this is a big problem in the world that we should be working on. I wanted to use population numbers but all the data is in percentages which dividing by population could be weird and not good data. Using a map representation for my visualizations was the best decision since you can clearly see the difference by countries and regions more easily than having to think where different countries are located.
